##1. Project Title
Optimizing IT Support Team Performance Using Analytics

## 1. Problem Statement
IT support teams handle a large volume of service tickets daily. Delays in response and resolution times, inconsistent service quality, frequent escalations, and uneven workload distribution can significantly impact operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.
One of the major challenges in IT support operations is the lack of data-driven visibility into team performance. Without measurable insights, managers face difficulties in identifying performance gaps, operational bottlenecks, workload imbalance, and areas that require improvement.
This project aims to apply data analytics techniques to evaluate IT support operations and monitor key performance indicators (KPIs). By analyzing ticket data and service metrics, the project generates actionable insights that can help improve service efficiency, optimize team performance, and enhance customer satisfaction.

## 2. Data Set Description
The dataset used in this project represents IT support ticket records, where each row corresponds to a single support request raised by a user.
The dataset contains operational and service-related information that helps analyze support team efficiency and service quality.
Key Attributes in the Dataset
Ticket ID – Unique identifier assigned to each support ticket
Ticket Created Date – Date and time when the ticket was raised
Ticket Closed Date – Date and time when the issue was resolved
Priority Level – Severity level of the issue (Low, Medium, High, Critical)
Category – Type of issue (Hardware, Software, Network, Security, etc.)
Assigned Agent – Support staff member responsible for resolving the ticket
Response Time – Time taken by the support agent to respond to the ticket
Resolution Time – Total time taken to fully resolve the issue
Ticket Status – Current state of the ticket (Open, Closed, Escalated)
Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) – Feedback rating provided by the user after ticket resolution
The dataset structure is suitable for performance evaluation, operational analysis, and dashboard visualization.

## 3. KPIs
To evaluate IT support team performance, the following KPIs are analyzed:
*Average Resolution Time (ART)
Measures the average time taken to resolve support tickets. Lower resolution times indicate higher operational efficiency.
*Average First Response Time (FRT)
Represents how quickly support agents respond to newly created tickets. Faster responses improve customer satisfaction and service quality.
*Ticket Volume
Total number of tickets received within a specific time period (daily, weekly, or monthly). This helps analyze workload trends and demand patterns.
*SLA Compliance Rate
Percentage of tickets resolved within the agreed Service Level Agreement (SLA) time frame. This metric indicates how well the support team meets service commitments.
*Escalation Rate
Measures the proportion of tickets that require escalation to higher-level support teams. High escalation rates may indicate skill gaps or complex issue categories.
*Agent Productivity
Number of tickets resolved by each support agent. This metric helps evaluate individual performance and identify workload imbalance.
*Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)
Represents customer feedback after ticket resolution. Higher CSAT scores indicate better service quality and customer experience.

## 5. Key Insights
Based on the analysis of the dataset, the following insights can be derived:
High-priority tickets tend to have longer resolution times compared to lower-priority tickets.
Certain support agents handle a significantly higher number of tickets, indicating possible workload imbalance.
Escalations are more common in specific issue categories such as network-related problems.
SLA compliance rates tend to decline during peak ticket periods.
Faster response times show a positive correlation with higher customer satisfaction scores.

## 6. Recommendations
Based on the insights obtained from the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:
Redistribute workload more evenly among support agents to prevent burnout and improve efficiency.
Provide targeted training programs for categories that experience higher escalation rates.
Strengthen SLA monitoring systems during peak workload periods.
Implement automated ticket routing systems to assign tickets based on agent expertise and issue category.
Establish clear performance benchmarks for response time and resolution time.
These improvements can significantly enhance operational performance and improve customer service quality.
